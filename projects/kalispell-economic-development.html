Economic Development Mapping Project

Using ArcGIS Pro to Identify Zoning Areas and Development Opportunities
Kalispell, Montana
GIS Analysis Urban Planning ArcGIS Pro Economic Development

Project Overview

This project utilized ArcGIS Pro to create thematic maps identifying business zones, residential areas, and parkland in Kalispell, Montana. The analysis aimed to help potential employers in this tourist town identify appropriate locations for business development, with particular attention to accessibility via major transportation routes.

Project Goals & Criteria

Primary Objectives:

  • Use polygon features to identify and categorize land use in Kalispell (businesses, residential, parkland)
  • Develop an appropriate color theme for clear visual distinction of different zones
  • Illustrate line features (roads and highways) with varying widths to represent their importance
  • Create a thematic map to guide business development decisions
  • Properly align coordinate systems using appropriate datums

Key Findings

Economic Activity Patterns

  • Central Business District: Most economic activity concentrates in the central part of Kalispell, at the intersection of US Highway 2 and US Highway 93
  • Healthcare Hub: Logan Health Medical Center is located directly north of Highway 2, off Highway 93
  • Residential Distribution: Residential areas primarily extend north along Highway 93
  • Highway Impact: The town's economic vitality is significantly enhanced by the presence of two major highways

Development Opportunities

  • Prime development locations align closely with major highways (Routes 2 and 93)
  • Strategic positioning near highways provides access to tourists visiting Glacier National Park and Whitefish Mountain Ski Resort
  • Restaurant and hospitality businesses would benefit most from these high-traffic corridors

Technical Methodology

Coordinate System & Datums

To properly display Flathead County (where Kalispell is located), the project required matching Montana's County Layer with the appropriate datum. The North American Albers Equal Area Conic USGS was identified as the matching datum for this region.

Note: Datums are standard measurements of latitude and longitude used to identify coordinates. Since datums aren't unified globally, selecting the correct one is crucial for accurate geographic mapping.

Cartographic Design Decisions

Scale Selection: A smaller scale was chosen to avoid interference with map features while maintaining readability. Given that the United States measures distance in miles, the scale utilizes imperial units rather than metric.

Visual Hierarchy: Highways were displayed with greater width than residential streets to emphasize their importance to transportation and commerce.

Comparative Analysis: Kalispell vs. New York City

To extend the analysis, I compared Kalispell's zoning patterns with New York City, where I live. Despite an 8 million person population difference, interesting structural similarities emerged.

Kalispell, Montana

  • Central business district at highway intersection
  • Economic growth driven by highways
  • Residential areas in northern sections
  • Tourism-focused economy
  • Limited public transit infrastructure

New York City

  • Central business district in Midtown
  • Economic growth driven by subway system
  • Residential areas in northern sections (e.g., uptown)
  • Diverse economy
  • Extensive public transit network

Key Insight: Despite vast differences in size and infrastructure, both cities demonstrate how transportation networks (whether highways or subways) serve as the backbone of economic development, with major business districts forming at transportation hubs and residential areas extending outward from these centers.

Course Integration & Learning Outcomes

This project built upon concepts from Geography 482, particularly the importance of coordinate systems and datums in accurate geographic analysis. An additional extra credit assignment reinforced how highway access is crucial to economic growth, especially in non-urban areas.

While urban areas often emphasize subways and buses as drivers of economic growth, this analysis demonstrates that highways play an equally vital role, particularly for communities in less densely populated regions that depend on automobile transportation and tourism.
