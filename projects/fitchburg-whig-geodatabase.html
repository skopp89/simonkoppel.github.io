Fitchburg, MA Whig Party Geodatabase & Outreach Analysis

I developed a comprehensive geodatabase to streamline voter registration data and inform outreach strategy. Using SQL, I converted ethnicity and party affiliation attributes into binary formats, applied normalization to remove duplicates, and produced census-block maps to identify registration gaps and target communities.

Tools: SQL • Geodatabase Design • GIS Mapping Geography 484 (Lesson 6) Location: Fitchburg, Massachusetts

Project Overview

This project combines database cleaning and spatial analysis to support an evidence-based voter outreach plan. I mapped voter registration and party affiliation by census block, then compared those patterns with Hispanic registration and Hispanic population share to highlight where multilingual engagement would be most effective.

  • Goal: Improve the reliability and usability of voter registration data for outreach planning.
  • Database work: SQL transformations (binary encoding) + normalization to reduce duplicates.
  • Spatial outputs: Thematic maps and proportional symbol mapping by census blocks.

These maps were used together to identify where registration lagged and where outreach could be better tailored.

Findings & Strategy

  • Registration gaps: Larger rural blocks showed weaker registration, suggesting outreach should include travel/transportation support to reach more distant areas. (PDF p.3)
  • Competitive blocks: Party affiliation patterns indicated where the Whigs could gain advantage by focusing on low-turnout or under-registered blocks with persuadable voters. (PDF p.3)
  • Spanish-language outreach: Based on Hispanic registration and population distribution, the strategy recommended multilingual (Spanish) flyers to engage Fitchburg’s growing Hispanic community. (PDF p.4)

What I’d Improve Next
