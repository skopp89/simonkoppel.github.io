My Story

From community service to spatial analysis

Before I became a GIS analyst, I spent nearly a decade working directly with communities in need. That experience shaped how I approach data today.

As an AmeriCorps member with Habitat for Humanity NYC, I led construction crews on Staten Island and Brooklyn, helping residents rebuild their homes after Hurricane Sandy. For two years, I showed up in all weather conditions, completing eight home repair projects alongside the families who lived in them.

For five years with Rescuing Leftover Cuisine, I led volunteer teams collecting approximately 360 pounds of surplus food weekly from New York businesses and delivering it to shelters. This taught me about logistics, consistency, and the importance of showing up.

At Brooklyn Workforce Innovations, I helped promote free job training programs for low-income New Yorkers, conducting outreach to community organizations and managing social media communications.

"I bring work ethic, persistence, attention to detail, and genuine care for community impact to every GIS project. You get someone who shows up, follows through, and does the work right."

Today, as a researcher with Survivors to Superheroes, I develop maps of crisis centers and mental health resources across New York State, helping survivors of sexual violence locate critical services. It's work that combines everything I've learned: technical GIS skills, community understanding, and the drive to make information accessible to people who need it.
